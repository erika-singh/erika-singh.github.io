001
AI Education Workshop Clear Communication Translating Complexity Presentation

Simplified complex AI concepts into an accessible workshop, securing adoption and presenting to a 20+ person strategic team.

›

Why I did this: I noticed a growing interest in AI across teams, also a gap in understanding how it could be applied meaningfully in daily work. Most conversations stayed either too technical or too vague to be actionable, and most people were unsure about the legalities and privacy implications of using AI.

Step 1

Built foundational understanding

I started by understanding how large language models actually work. I learnt the technical language first so that I could simplify it for others later.

Step 2

Identified where AI could create value

Instead of starting with tools, I started with the team's goals and workflows. I broke down how work was currently being done and looked for points where AI could realistically improve efficiency or insight.

Step 3

Structured the problem into two parts

I realized the challenge was twofold: people needed to understand AI, and people needed to see how to use it. So I split the workshop into two sections: education and application.

Highlights
Synthetic data

Using synthetic data to enable testing and modelling without exposing sensitive information.

Persona-based engagement

Building a "persona bank" to simulate audience reactions. For example, modelling how a specific alumni profile might respond to content to improve engagement strategies.

Results

This work contributed to the formation of an AI task force within the department, focused on developing practical applications of AI.

002
Customer Retention Strategy Data-Driven Decision Making Customer Insights Strategy

Identified high potential customers through a predictive analytics model, enabling targeted marketing and improved ticket sales.

›

Why I did this: A local theatre in Kingston, ON, the Screening Room operates in a highly competitive entertainment market, facing pressure from large cinema chains and streaming platforms. A key challenge was increasing repeat customers without wasting marketing resources on broad, unfocused campaigns. So, I built a model for a class project.

Problem
Statement
The Screening Room faces the challenge of increasing repeat customers in a competitive entertainment market dominated by large cinema chains and streaming services.
Core
Issues
Difficulty in identifying factors influencing customer loyalty and repeat visits
Pressure from larger cinema chains and the increasing popularity of streaming services
Potential inaccuracies and incompleteness in the available data including external factors influencing customer behavior
Inefficiencies
to solve
Marketing Budget Spend
Customer Experience
Targeted Promotions
Optimized Movie Selection
Targeted marketing efforts reduce wasted advertising spend on customers unlikely to return
Understanding customer preferences allows for customized movie recommendations and incentives, enhancing satisfaction
Customized promotions can draw customers back to The Screening Room despite competition from streaming services and larger cinema chain
The model can inform decisions about which genres and types of films to screen, maximizing audience engagement
Building the model

What I built

I developed a classification model to predict whether a customer would return, using behavioral and demographic data. This enabled segmentation based on return likelihood, allowing the business to move from broad campaigns to targeted outreach.

From insight to action

Before & After

Before: Promotions were applied broadly, with limited visibility into which customers were likely to return.
After: Customer segmentation enabled targeted outreach, focusing efforts on high-value customers and improving marketing efficiency.

Expanded impact

Beyond marketing

The model also supported decisions beyond marketing, including identifying popular genres, optimizing scheduling, and improving resource planning.

Results: The project demonstrated that targeted retention strategies could significantly improve efficiency, with projections showing positive ROI within five months of implementation.
